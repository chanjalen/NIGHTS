# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary: the stranger in a new city — traveler, recent mover, or anyone outside their usual neighborhood. Searching on their phone, probably at night, in a context where they need a fast, trusted answer. The emotional state is anticipatory: they want to go out, they don't know where, and they have no local to ask.

Secondary: locals who want to discover something new in their own city, or contribute ratings for places they know.

## Product Purpose

NITE answers three questions the stranger actually needs: what's the scene like in this city, which bar fits my vibe tonight, and is it worth going there right now. It does this through community ratings from people who were physically there (verified by check-in), live crowd counts, and vibe/music tags that let users filter by what they're actually looking for. The goal is to be the trusted insider voice when you don't have insider friends.

## Brand Personality

Dark, direct, urban. Confident without being flashy. Feels like the city at 10pm — not a tourist guide, not a promoter. The voice of someone who actually goes out.

## Anti-references

Yelp and Google Maps: generic utility platforms that treat bars like dentist offices. Clinical, no vibe, no nightlife-specific context. NITE should never feel like a directory or a review aggregator. It should feel like a specific, trusted community — not an algorithm.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust over hype.** The ratings are from people who were there. The UI should make that credibility visible — verified badges, check-in counts, honest signal — not sell the experience before the user decides.
2. **Speed beats completeness.** The user is on their phone, possibly standing outside, maybe a little drunk. Get them to the right venue fast. The answer to "which bar tonight" must be reachable in three taps.
3. **The night is the context.** The ambient experience should feel like nighttime — dark surfaces, contained light, energy without noise. Not a productivity tool. Not a tourist pamphlet.
4. **Community, not algorithm.** Every piece of content on NITE came from a person. The design should surface that humanity — real names, verified presence, honest tags — not smooth it into anonymized aggregates.
5. **Mobile is the primary surface.** Users are on their phones, outdoors, in low-light conditions. Touch targets, text contrast, and one-handed nav are not accessibility extras — they are the core use case.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text). Mobile-first touch targets (minimum 44px). Readable in dark ambient environments — err toward higher contrast rather than muted tones. Reduced motion support for the animated background. No reliance on color alone for state communication.
